Beef Suya (The Ultimate Street Food)

Suya is more than just grilled meat; it's an experience. Thinly sliced beef is marinated in Yaji—a spicy, nutty dry rub made from ginger, paprika, and ground peanuts—then flame-grilled to smoky perfection. It is the gold standard of Nigerian snacks.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb Flank or Sirloin Steak, sliced paper-thin against the grain
  • 1 cup Yaji Spice Mix (Ground kuli-kuli/peanuts, ginger, cayenne, garlic powder, onion powder, and bouillon)
  • 1/2 cup Vegetable Oil (to baste)
  • Skewers (soaked in water to prevent burning)
  • Garnish: Sliced red onions, tomatoes, and extra Yaji on the side

Prepeparation steps

  1. Slice the Meat

    Ensure the beef is sliced as thin as possible. If it's too thick, it won't get that classic "Suya" texture

  2. The Rub

    Dredge each slice of meat into yaji spice mix, pressinf firmly until every inch is coated in the orange-brown dust.

  3. Thread

    Weave the meat onto the skewersina flat, accordian-like fashion.

  4. Marinate

    Let the skewers sit for at least 30 minutes (or 2 hours in the fridge) to let the spices penetrate.

  5. Grill

    Place on a hot grill (or oven rack at 200°C). Brush the meat lightly with oil to keep it moist.

  6. Flip

    Grill for 10-15 minutes, flipping halfway through and basting again. You want charred edges but tender centers.

  7. The Reveal

    Serve hot on a sheet of newspaper or foil, topped with plenty of raw onions and tomatoes.
